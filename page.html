Basic Computing Skills for Seniors

Understanding a Computer

A computer is an electronic device that allows you to type documents, send email, browse the internet, and more. It includes a screen (monitor), a keyboard, and a mouse.

Turning On and Off

To turn on a computer, press the power button. It may take a few moments to load. To turn it off, click the "Start" menu, then choose "Shut Down".

Using a Mouse

The mouse helps you move a pointer on the screen. Clicking means pressing the left button on the mouse once. Double-clicking means pressing it twice quickly. Right-clicking shows more options.

Using a Keyboard

The keyboard allows you to type letters, numbers, and symbols. Each key represents a character. The "Enter" key is used to move to the next line or submit something.

Opening Programs

Programs like web browsers (Google Chrome), word processors (Microsoft Word), and games can be opened by clicking their icons on the desktop or start menu.

Going Online

To use the internet, click on a browser like Chrome or Edge. You can type what you're looking for into the search bar and press Enter to find information.

Typing and Saving Documents

Open a program like Notepad or Word. Start typing using your keyboard. To save your work, click on "File" and then "Save". Choose a name and a place to store your file.

Using Email

You can send and receive messages with email. Open a browser, go to your email provider (like Gmail), and log in. To write a message, click "Compose", type the address, subject, and message, then click "Send".

Practice Tips

  • Try typing your name and saving the file.
  • Use the mouse to click on different icons.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you try sending an email.

With practice, using a computer becomes easier every day!
