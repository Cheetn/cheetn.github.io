How Can you Avoid Scams

Common Online Scams

Scammers use emails, messages, and fake websites to steal your personal information. Here are some common scams:

  • Phishing Emails – Emails pretending to be from banks or companies, asking you to provide sensitive information.
  • Fake Lotteries – Messages claiming you won a prize and requesting a payment to claim it.
  • Tech Support Scams – Calls or pop-ups pretending to be from Microsoft or Apple, asking for remote access to your device.
  • Investment Fraud – Schemes promising high returns with little risk, often using pressure tactics.
  • Romance Scams – Fake online relationships where the scammer asks for money.

How to Protect Yourself

Follow these steps to stay safe online:

  • Verify the Source – If you receive an unexpected email or message, contact the company directly.
  • Never Click Suspicious Links – Hover over links to check their destination before clicking.
  • Use Strong Passwords – Avoid simple passwords; use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication – Adds an extra layer of security to your accounts.
  • Be Skeptical of Urgent Requests – Scammers pressure you to act quickly; take your time to verify.
  • Consult Family or Friends – If something feels off, ask someone you trust before taking action.

How to Identify Scam Messages

Here are some signs of a scam message:

  • Bad Grammar or Spelling Mistakes – Many scam messages have errors.
  • Strange or Unfamiliar Senders – If you don’t know the sender, be cautious.
  • Requests for Personal Information – Legitimate companies never ask for sensitive details via email or message.
  • Unrealistic Promises – Be wary of offers that seem too good to be true.
  • Threats or Urgency – Scammers often claim your account will be locked unless you act immediately.

What to Do If You Are Scammed

If you suspect you have been scammed:

  • Stop communicating with the scammer immediately.
  • Report the scam to your bank if you sent money.
  • Change your passwords and secure your accounts.
  • Contact authorities or consumer protection agencies.
  • Inform family members to prevent further incidents.
