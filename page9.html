Internet Basics for Seniors

What is the Internet?

The Internet is a global network that allows people to connect, find information, communicate, and entertain themselves. You can think of it like a giant library with answers to almost anything.

Connecting to the Internet

  • Make sure your computer or device is turned on.
  • Click on the Wi-Fi icon (usually in the bottom right corner of the screen).
  • Select your Wi-Fi network from the list and enter the password.
  • Once connected, you can start browsing the web.

Using a Web Browser

A browser is a program that lets you access websites. Common browsers include:

  • Google Chrome
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari (on Apple devices)

How to Search the Web

  • Open your browser.
  • Click on the address bar at the top.
  • Type in what you're looking for, like "how to bake a cake".
  • Press the Enter key.
  • You’ll see a list of websites with information about your topic.

Staying Safe Online

Online safety means protecting yourself from dangers on the internet. Here are important tips:

  • Use strong passwords: Combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using your name or birthdate.
  • Think before you click: Never click suspicious links or attachments in emails or messages.
  • Keep personal info private: Don't share things like your address, phone number, or bank info unless the site is trustworthy.
  • Look for secure websites: They start with "https://" and show a padlock icon.
  • Update regularly: Keep your device, browser, and apps up to date with the latest versions.
  • Ask for help: If you're unsure about something, ask a trusted family member or friend.

Quiz 1: Can You Spot the Scam?

Question: You receive an email that says you've won $10,000, and it asks for your bank account number. What should you do?

  • A. Reply and give your bank account number
  • B. Click the link in the email to claim the prize
  • C. Ignore the email and report it as spam

Answer: C — If something sounds too good to be true, it probably is.

Quiz 2: Password Safety

Question: Which of the following is the strongest password?

  • A. mary123
  • B. password
  • C. M@ry2025!

Answer: C — It uses a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

Quiz 3: Is This Website Safe?

Question: You're about to shop online. What should you check before entering your credit card information?

  • A. The website looks colorful
  • B. There's a padlock icon and the address starts with "https://"
  • C. The site offers a big discount

Answer: B — Only enter your payment details on secure websites.

With practice, you'll feel more confident using the internet for learning, fun, and staying connected with others!
